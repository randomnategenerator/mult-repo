All About Harbor Seals!

Introduction

Harbor Seals are a kind of seal found in the Northern Hemisphere. They are the most common kind of seal, being found in the Atlantic and Pacific oceans. Harbor seals can be brown, white, or grey, and are distinguished by their V-shaped nostrils.The blubber under a seal's skin helps it to stay warm in the cool habitats they live in.
I chose them for this assignment because I think they are very cute! Let's dive into all there is know about harbor seals!

Different Kinds

There are five main subspecies of harbor seal:

  • Eastern Atlantic common seal

  • Western Atlantic common seal

  • Ungava seal

  • Pacific harbor seal

  • Insular Seal

Population

The worldwide seal population is estimated at 350,000-500,000. While the overall population is not threatened, the populations in Greenland, Hokkaido and the Baltic Sea are exceptions. Local populations are threatened by disease and human conflict, but there is good news! Commercial hunting of seals is illegal in many parts of the world, and people are usually instructed to leave seals alone unless they are in danger. Unfortunately, many seals fall victim to bycatch, where they are caught by fishing nets accidentally.
Seal populations vary by area. Along the pacific coast many seals can be found, especially along the shore. On the Atlantic coast, however, seal populations have been wiped out by humans shooting them on sight. Bounties were placed on the head of every seal shot, incentivizing overhunting. Pollution from cities also affected wildlife populations, seen especially in New York City and Boston. However, seals have begun to return these areas as of 2020! Pockets of the Marine coast and Canada never wiped out the seals, so they have begun to migrate to these areas.

Behaviour & Diet

Harbor seals are solitary creatures, but often outgoing during breeding season. They do not form as large groups as other seals, but often haul out to sea to rest. They can be hesitant to haul out when humans are around, so researchers must be careful when studying harbor seals. They are regarded as more vocally recitent, using vocalizations to mark territory and attract mates. Habror seals often frequent the same resting spots, spending many days at sea in search of food. Common gathering places include harbors (of course), bays, and sandy intertidal areas.
Some of the harbor seal's favourite foods are salmon, anchovies, sea bass, cod, shrimp, crabs, squids, and many more! Atlantic subspecies also enjoy deeper venturing for differnet kinds of fish. They've even been seen to eat ducks!

Andre was a seal adopted by Harry Goodridge, a tree surgeon in Maine. Harry raised Andre as a pup, expecting him to return to the wild as he grew up. Instead, Andre chose to stay with Harry until he died! This true story was portrayed in the 1994 film Andre. So cute!!!

Popeye

Popeye was a seal famous for her cloudy left eye. This is where her name came from, reminding tourists of Popeye the Sailor. The Port of Friday Harbor named her their official seal and made a granite sculpture of her, which still remains at the harbor!
